About Me

I am a researcher at the Dutch National Applied Research Organization (TNO-ESI) and a guest researcher at Eindhoven University of Technology. My work lies at the intersection of empirical software engineering, legacy system modernization, and open-source ecosystems. I study software repositories, conduct surveys and interviews, and explore both social and technical aspects of software development in industry and open-source contexts. I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from TU Eindhoven in 2023, where I researched logs and models in engineering complex embedded production software systems.

This illustration, used as the cover of my Ph.D. thesis, depicts a curious engineer examining data such as logs generated by industrial production systems. That engineer is me — both literally and figuratively. I analyze data from these systems and conduct empirical research to understand how engineers work, the tools they use, and how we can improve tools, processes, and collaboration in modern software engineering.
